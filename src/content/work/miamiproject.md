The Miami Project to Cure Paralysis is a research center at the <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">University of Miami Miller School of Medicine</span>, where researchers develop novel therapies for paralysis resulting from <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">spinal cord injury</span>. Research areas include <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">neuroplasticity</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">neuroprotection</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">neuroregeneration</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">cell therapy</span>, and <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">neural engineering</span>, among others. I was a member of the <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">Neural Stem and Tissue Engineered Microenvironments (NeuSTEM)</span> laboratory, where research focuses on the development and characterization of engineered <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">nanoparticles</span> for regenerative medicine applications.

- Dissected brain and spinal cord tissue from mice for <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">CNS myelin debris</span> isolation, including homogenization and <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">density gradient centrifugation</span>.
- Developed and conducted <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">nanoparticle treatment studies</span>, performing <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">DMMB</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">MTS</span>, and <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">BCA assays</span>.
- Fabricated and characterized experimental <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">biomaterials</span> (nanoparticles and microspheres), including <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">enzymatic depolymerization</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">ultrafiltration</span>, lyophilization, and <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">zeta potential analysis</span>.
- Synthesized <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">chondroitin sulfate nanoparticles (CS-NPs)</span> and evaluated fabrication parameters to optimize yield and surface charge.
- Supported <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">in vivo wound-healing studies</span>, including assisting in <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">rat surgeries</span> and image processing using <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">Fiji (ImageJ)</span>.
- Used <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">aseptic technique</span> to maintain cultures of <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">Schwann cells</span>, <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">fibroblasts</span>, and <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">iBMDMs</span>.
- Evaluated cell culture purity and performed <span class="font-semibold dark:font-bold dark:text-white">cell viability assays</span> for Schwann cell cultures.
